# Compliance and Regulatory Standards

## Overview

Job-Magician is designed to meet all applicable state and federal workforce development, vocational rehabilitation, and accessibility requirements. This document outlines our compliance framework.

## Federal Compliance

### Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)
- **Purpose**: Federal workforce development legislation requiring integrated service delivery
- **Compliance Areas**:
  - Pre-Employment Transition Services for youth with disabilities (ages 14-22)
  - Comprehensive vocational rehabilitation services
  - Performance accountability and reporting
  - Integrated resource planning with state workforce boards
  - Data collection and outcome tracking

### Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- **Title I**: Equal employment opportunity compliance
- **Title II**: State and local government services accessibility
- **Title III**: Public accommodations and commercial facilities
- **Implementation**: 
  - Reasonable accommodation request workflows
  - Non-discrimination policies
  - Accessible digital platforms (WCAG 2.1 Level AA)

### Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act
- **Requirement**: Federal agencies must make their electronic and information technology accessible
- **Implementation**:
  - Screen reader compatibility
  - Keyboard navigation support
  - Alternative text for images
  - Accessible forms and interactive elements
  - Color contrast compliance

### Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA
- **Perceivable**: Information presented in ways users can perceive
- **Operable**: UI components and navigation must be operable
- **Understandable**: Information and operation must be understandable
- **Robust**: Content must be robust enough for assistive technologies

### Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
- **Compliance**: Employer guidance on wage and hour requirements
- **Documentation**: Employment eligibility verification (I-9)

### Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
- **Application**: When medical/psychological evaluations are involved
- **Safeguards**:
  - Protected Health Information (PHI) encryption
  - Role-based access controls
  - Audit logging
  - Business Associate Agreements with providers

### Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)
- **Application**: When working with students in transition services
- **Protection**: Educational records privacy and parental rights

## State Compliance (Texas)

### Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) Standards
- **Authority**: Texas state vocational rehabilitation services administration
- **Requirements**:
  - Provider certification and standards (VR-SFP Manual)
  - Contract compliance and monitoring
  - Financial accountability for state and federal funds
  - Quality assurance and continuous improvement

### Texas Administrative Code
- **Chapter 801**: Vocational Rehabilitation Services rules
- **Chapter 819**: Employment Services requirements
- **Chapter 809**: WIOA program administration

### Texas Government Code
- **Chapter 2308**: Workforce development programs
- **Chapter 2309**: Skills development fund
- **Compliance**: State procurement and contracting requirements

## Data Security and Privacy

### Data Protection Measures
1. **Encryption**: 
   - Data at rest: AES-256 encryption
   - Data in transit: TLS 1.3
2. **Access Control**:
   - Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
   - Role-based access control (RBAC)
   - Principle of least privilege
3. **Audit Logging**:
   - Comprehensive activity logs
   - Regular security audits
   - Incident response procedures
4. **Data Retention**:
   - Compliance with federal record retention requirements (minimum 3 years)
   - Secure data disposal protocols

### Privacy Standards
- **GDPR Alignment**: Data protection principles applied universally
- **CCPA Compliance**: California consumer privacy rights
- **Transparency**: Clear privacy notices and consent mechanisms
- **User Rights**: Data access, correction, and deletion requests

## Program Eligibility and Service Standards

### Vocational Rehabilitation Eligibility
1. **Primary Criteria**:
   - Individual has a physical or mental impairment
   - Impairment creates a substantial barrier to employment
   - Individual requires VR services to prepare for, obtain, retain, or advance in employment
   - Individual can benefit from VR services in terms of an employment outcome

2. **Documentation Requirements**:
   - Medical/psychological documentation
   - Educational records (as applicable)
   - Work history
   - Assessment results

### Service Delivery Standards
- **Individualized Plan for Employment (IPE)**: Developed collaboratively with client
- **Informed Choice**: Client participation in service selection
- **Comparable Benefits**: Coordination with other funding sources
- **Trial Work Experience**: Opportunities to explore career options

## Reporting and Accountability

### Performance Metrics (WIOA)
1. Employment Rate (2nd & 4th quarter after exit)
2. Median Earnings
3. Credential Attainment
4. Measurable Skill Gains

### Reporting Requirements
- **Federal**: RSA-911 case service reports
- **State**: TWC quarterly performance reports
- **Local**: Workforce board coordination and updates

### Provider Standards
